Uses

Having a "Uses" page has now become Internet lore. It is an updated list of tools and gadgets that make my life easier on a daily or weekly basis.

Workhorses:

  • logos - my MacBook Pro (Retina, 15-inch, Late 2013). This computer is an absolute beast! I got it second hand and even after years of abusing it for development and fun, it still works better than most laptops out there. It also features the best keyboard Apple ever made.
  • kaizen - my MacBook Pro (15-inch, 2017). My wife tells me I type like I'm angry all the time and this computer's keyboard takes a daily punishment of endless emails and Slack messages.

Communication Tools:

  • Telegram - we use Telegram as our main communication tool internally and externally at Hiveterminal. It is simple, works everywhere and I love how the draft messages you start typing on the mobile, get transferred instantly to your other devices as well. Also, zero threads, which I love.
  • Slack - we use Slack primarily at Briefd as well as conversations between the Hiveterminal and Kalmia development teams.
  • Google Workspace (formerly G-Suite) & Gmail - the best thing Google ever made, even if you include the search engine side. Having access to multiple emails in one browser window saves lives and electrons.

Writing:

  • Notion - I love the simplicity of Notion. The white background and the blank page feeling make it easy for me to write down an idea real quick or hammer out a blog post.
  • Hemingway Editor - I usually throw stuff in Hemingway editor before they get published. Helps me visualize the horrible long sentences and break them down.
  • Grammarly - everybody makes typos, mixes in the wrong words, half-deletes a sentence, and uses commas where there shouldn't be any. Grammarly catches at least half and saves you the embarrassment of having to apologize for an email written on the fly.

Development:

  • VSCode - I do most of my hobby development work in VSCode. Free and beats most IDEs by a long shot, although the IntelliSense for PHP isn't as good or intuitive as in PHPStorm.
  • Fira Code - the font I use in VSCode. Makes even my $h!t code readable as f*ck.
  • Slime - I've enjoyed "close-to-Matrix" color themes for most of my development life and Slime is perfect. Easy on the eyes, yet still providing enough color to be able to scan the code quickly.
  • Brave Browser - I've been an early adopter of Brave Browser and I can't imagine my life without it. At the time of writing this, it reports 300754 blocked trackers & ads, 5.41GB in bandwidth and 4.2 hours of time saved due to faster load times.
  • Sequel Ace - developed as a successor to the now-abandoned SequelPro. For the users of its' predecessor, offers a familiar interface with fewer bugs.
  • iTerm2 - I deny knowledge and/or existence of any other terminal app on Mac. Complete with zsh shell. The person that developed the Cmd + click to make links clickable, deserves a medal of honor.
  • Github - by opening up the team features and with the addition of supporting tools, such as Github Actions, Github has become my go-to version control hosting provider. Used by Briefd, Hiveterminal and for my own open-source work.

Domain purchases:

  • Domenca - domain registrar, based in Slovenia. All local domains acquired here.
  • Namecheap - for domains I can't find on Domenca, Namecheap to the rescue. Also has a really cool logo and color scheme generator I occasionally use for side-projects and zero-budget projects.

Productivity

  • LastPass - The last time I had to remember my password, was in 2019. The rest is stored within the LastPass walls. Works well on mobile and desktop. Sadly, no longer entirely free.
