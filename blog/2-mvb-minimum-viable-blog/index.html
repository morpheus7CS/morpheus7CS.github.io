MVB: Minimum Viable Blog

by
Gal Jakič

It took me two years to go from buying the jakic.dev domain and turning it into a blog. The reason was obvious, simple and didn't have anything to do with tech or work.

As developers, our job is not (only) to write code. We spend most of our professional lives translating business requirements to code, tools, and products. Creating abstractions. Turning concepts into models, migrations, controllers, and endpoints. We turn ideas into flowcharts and prototypes and make beautiful products, (ideally) on time and on budget.

We get to play with all sorts of tech. From basic to advanced. From great to the one that makes you cringe.

This is why it is an absolute nightmare to try to design and publish your own personal site. The voice in your head tells you it is not polished enough. Or wide enough. Or colorful. Or full of content. Well, you get the idea...

That it needs more references and client testimonials. It also tells you your front-end skills are inadequate. That you should hire someone to code it up for you, or at least review it. That you need to get a proper photo taken and use one of the technologies from the razor's edge to build it. Maybe it should be serverless? Maybe it should use GraphQL to return your content?

I'm not describing a fictional voice. This voice was in my head the whole time when building gal.jakic.dev, where this article will reside.

I almost gave in and listened. But then I remembered:

This voice is not real. This voice is the reason that I don't have a personal web page, even after 15 years of working on the web. This voice is just imposter syndrome talking. Just ship the f*cking thing already...

There is this thing called WordPress you could use...

Yes and no. It's not that the features of MVB will be "too advanced for WordPress to handle". But every time I type laravel new, it's a magical feeling. I don't get that with WordPress. And we all need more magic in our lives.

How about Jigsaw from Tighten?

Jigsaw is hands down one of the best products out there. I only have one issue with Jigsaw: I wish its folder structure mirrored Laravel's. That would make it much easier for me to get started and not have to re-map the concepts in my head.

So, what will you use?

So I built myself an MVB - a minimum viable blog. It is a Laravel app, with Tailwind CSS and Alpine.js. It has a Home page. It has a Blog page. It has an About page. I built it myself and it is good enough.

There are a million things I wish were on it and are not yet there. But as long as I keep writing, they will pop up eventually.

If you got this far, thank you.
