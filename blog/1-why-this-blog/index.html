Why This Blog?

by
Gal Jakič

A Hello-World-like blog post with an equally lame title, about why this blog exists and what I would like to do with it going forward.

I'm just a regular guy, trying to help startups and their teams succeed in a challenging environment. Apart from that, I have a wife (Nina), a son (Leo) and a dog (Lego). I have an opportunity to work full time on two magnificent projects: AutoBrief, an intelligent future-proof solution to improve used-car sales and Hiveterminal, a blockchain-based invoice financing company. It comes with two teams I love and a never-ending menu of challenges I love solving.

Even after 15 years of working with web technologies and 5+ years of working in tech on a daily basis, I am still fascinated by the simple things. Like the fact that I can give you my exact age as you're reading this, which is days, without needing to update the page every single day.

We own a car and in a few years, my wife will own the 52 square meter flat we live in. We are lucky to live in a quiet part of town, in a country - which you should visit, should you ever get the chance - where you don't have to worry about crime rates, or healthcare bills, or being able to send your children to college. Incidentally, the same country that gave the World Luka Dončič. (Slovenia. And you are welcome.)

In 2002, I suffered a spinal stroke and have a wheelchair to prove it. It allowed me to do unimaginable things: spend the next 10 years of my life focused on Para-Alpine Ski Racing, being part of Slovene Para-Alpine Ski Team, and representing my country in 3 Paralympic Winter Games: 2006 Torino, 2010 Vancouver, and 2014 Sochi - if you are a nerd for details, such as me.

During that time, I was also exposed to some other amazing opportunities: becoming the 1st skier with a disability to ski down the 2nd largest ski jumping hill in the World in a straight line. Crossing Slovenia on a hand bike in 25 hours. Being a TEDx speaker. Being named Friend of Wings For Life Foundation (along with many exceptional athletes, among them Felix Baumgartner, the man who - literally - jumped from the edge of Space). Working as a motivational speaker for established brands in Slovenia and Europe, trying to inspire the up-and-coming developers on meetups (and perhaps soon, conferences). Professionally working with and for, many talented developers, designers, and business owners.

But it's the other side of the coin that sent me on a journey. If life - or more specifically, my own spinal stroke or two of my wife's breast cancer diagnosis - have taught me anything is that you should take nothing for granted.

I am by no account perfect. I'm at my best in challenging situations, or as I like to put it, long-distance suffering. I'm also my own worst enemy when the landscape is perfect. I tend to overthink, over-engineer, and subsequently discard ideas, before even giving them a chance to succeed. I still fear front-end code and have a real problem, when trying to write the first page in a blank notebook someone bought for me.

I hope to get exactly 3 things out of this:

  • talk about lessons learned in startup land
  • give you a Behind-The-Scenes, Zero-Bull$h!t at some of the stuff we get to do there
  • (still don't know and that's OK.)

Follow along if this sounds like something you would be interested in.

Gal Jakič

Project & Product Manager at Briefd and Project Delivery Lead at Hiveterminal. In 2006, he got started in tech by developing WordPress websites and got into tech full time in 2016. Between 2005 and 2014, he has been a member of the Slovene Para Alpine Ski Team and has represented his home country, Slovenia, in Paralympic Winter Games in 2006, 2010 and 2014.
